# Halen — Topical Authority Map

Goal: own the **fleur de sel / finishing salt** topic space, then radiate outward into
gourmet, culinary, and educational salt queries — funneling all of it to jar reservations.

**Architecture:** hub-and-spoke. Each cluster has one **pillar/hub** (broad, evergreen)
surrounded by **spokes** (specific intents) that link up to the hub and out to the product.

> **Volume bands are directional expert estimates (US monthly), not live data.**
> Validate with a keyword tool before committing budget. Bands:
> 🔵 High ≈ 5k+ · 🟢 Med ≈ 1–5k · 🟡 Low ≈ <1k
> Intent: **INFO** (top-funnel) · **COMM** (commercial investigation) · **TXN** (transactional) · **USE** (use-case, mid→bottom)
> Buyer proximity ★–★★★★★ · Difficulty for a *new* brand: Low / Med / High
> Priority: **P0** (first 90 days) → **P3** (months 9–12)

## Internal-linking rules (how authority flows)

- **Spoke → Hub**: every spoke links up to its cluster hub with descriptive anchor text.
- **Hub → Spokes**: each hub lists/links all its spokes (the pillar is a table of contents).
- **Everything → Product**: every page carries exactly one Reserve CTA + one contextual product link.
- **Money pages get the most inbound links**: funnel internal equity to collections / best-of /
  comparison pages — they convert. (`Best finishing salts`, `Gourmet salt`, `Fleur de sel vs Maldon`.)
- **Comparison pages** link to both entities compared *and* the product as the recommended pick.
- **Cross-cluster bridges**: e.g. `Best salt for steak` → `Finishing salt` hub + `Fleur de sel` hub.
- Add each new URL to [`sitemap.ts`](../../src/app/sitemap.ts) as it ships.

---

## Page-type templates (what each must contain)

- **Pillar/Hub**: 1,800–3,000 words, jump-link TOC, Article schema, links to all spokes, FAQ block.
- **Comparison**: a real spec table (origin, texture, additives, price, best-for), verdict, FAQ, both-entity + product links.
- **Use-case / recipe**: HowTo or Recipe schema, the "why this salt" angle, image, product CTA.
- **Collection / best-of**: honest criteria, your pick positioned (not faked #1), comparison table, TXN intent.
- **E-E-A-T (origin/maker)**: real author/maker attribution, photos, provenance — your trust moat.

Every page: one search intent, schema, links in + out, a Reserve CTA, and genuinely unique content
(no thin/templated spinning — Google's helpful-content system demotes it).
